# Workshops

The [handbook](/docs/getting-started) teaches the spec-driven-development lifecycle as a
self-serve exercise. **Workshops are the facilitated, cohort version** — you walk the same
lifecycle, but together and in real time, applied to your *own* project instead of a sandbox.

They exist for the parts that are hardest to learn alone: handing real work to Claude and
trusting it to run, with someone in the room when it goes sideways.

## Recommended pathway

Workshops assume you arrive prepared. The recommended sequence:

1. **Prep solo.** Work through [Getting Started](/docs/getting-started) (set up your
   workspace) and [Working with Claude](/docs/working-with-claude) (the lifecycle, end to
   end). You should be comfortable with spec → plan before a workshop, not learning it there.
2. **Bring a spec and a plan for your own work.** A workshop turns *your* plan into output —
   it isn't useful without one. If your plan isn't execution-ready, the
   [Plan stage](/docs/working-with-claude/plan) walks you through getting it there.
3. **Attend the build day.** [Building with Claude](./building-with-claude/index.md) is the
   in-person session where you execute that plan — roadmap it, sequence it, and run large
   chunks autonomously.

## Available workshops

- **[Building with Claude](./building-with-claude/index.md)** — a hands-on day executing your
  own plan with Claude. Five exercises, from decomposing a plan into a roadmap to running
  multi-task chunks autonomously.
