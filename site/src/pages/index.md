# A small research lab that runs on documented process

## The lab

The CAMELS Research Group (Conversational Agents in Multimodal Embedded
Latent Space) is a small, independent research group founded by Watson
Blair, about a year into its program. The group studies how conversational
agents can work with multiple modalities — speech, text, and the signals
around them — in a shared latent space, as part of a broader aim of
building toward beneficial AI. Its first application area is
language-practice tooling for learners of English as a second language.

## This handbook

lab-os is the lab's operating system: the conventions, onboarding
materials, and tooling that define how the lab works. That includes
commit, pull-request, and logging standards; data-protection rules;
document templates; the CI checks that enforce them; and a Claude-assisted
onboarding flow. This site is the readable version of those rules — the
same files the lab's repositories and automation consume.
